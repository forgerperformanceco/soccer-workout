# Yardsmith — Competitive Landscape & Differentiation

How Yardsmith is positioned against the existing golf-fitness app market: who's
out there, where the open field is, what we can legitimately borrow, what we must
avoid, and the features that close our one measurability gap.

> **Read the competitor reviews critically.** The most-cited "best golf fitness
> apps" rankings (e.g. DRVN's blog) are often published *by* the app that ranks #1.
> Treat their five-criteria rubric as a fair scorecard, but remember it's written to
> reward the author's own product and it omits the entire category we lead: nutrition.

---

## The market, in three lanes

Golf fitness/performance apps cluster into three groups — and **none of them occupy
the training × nutrition × AI-coach intersection Yardsmith is built for.**

| App | Lane | Does | Model | Gap |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **DRVN** | Athletic training | Golf-biomechanics strength programming, periodisation, a composite assessment ("Golf Fitness Handicap™") | Subscription (+ pro/facility tiers) | No nutrition. Coaching is programmed, not conversational. |
| **GolfForever** | Mobility / seniors | Low-intensity stability + mobility, injury prevention; anchored to a sold-separately Swing Trainer | Sub + hardware | Low athletic ceiling. No nutrition. Hardware-gated. |
| **Fitforgolf** | General fitness-for-golf | Stability/conditioning, decent library, some gym work | Sub | Less golf-specific; partial periodisation; no nutrition. |
| **JoeyD Golf** | Swing speed | Superspeed/overspeed distance protocols | Sub (+ aids) | Narrow — speed only. No strength breadth, no nutrition. |
| **TheStack / SuperSpeed** | Swing speed | Baseline-tested overspeed plans | Hardware + app | Speed only. No mass-building, no diet. |
| **Sportsbox AI / HackMotion / 18Birdies** | Swing **measurement** | 3D/sensor swing capture and metrics | Sub | Explicitly *measure, don't coach*. No fitness, no nutrition. |

### Two themes across the entire field
1. **Almost nobody does nutrition.** These are training or measurement apps. Fueling
   a 4–5 hour round *and* eating to build lean, fast mass is an open field — the
   leading reviews don't even list nutrition as an evaluation criterion.
2. **"AI" today means computer-vision swing analysis, not a coach.** Even the
   measurement leaders concede they output numbers and leave interpretation to the
   golfer or a paid instructor.

## Our differentiation (the wedge)

1. **Nutrition is the moat.** The only app turning a golfer's stats into daily macros,
   meal-by-meal carb timing around their tee/training slot, realistic meals, and
   on-course fueling. A whole pillar the competition skips.
2. **A coach, not a dashboard.** The AI coach reads *this user's* macro targets,
   training log, and speed trend and says "you're 15g under on carbs and your 7-iron
   speed stalled two weeks — here's the fix." The measurement apps admit they can't.
3. **Power-to-weight framing.** Build mass *as a means to speed*, with a Lean-Out
   phase — smarter than "just get bigger" or "just swing faster."
4. **Zero friction.** Runs in a browser on any phone, offline, free to start. No kit.

## What we can borrow vs. what we must avoid

**Borrow — industry-standard concepts and proven UX patterns (not anyone's IP):**
- **A single composite baseline score** a golfer improves toward (handicap-like). The
  *idea* isn't protectable — build our own from our own inputs (see below).
- **Baseline-test-then-personalise** (TheStack's framing) — we already do this; lean in.
- **Published sports science** — overspeed training, rotational power, ground-force,
  periodisation vocabulary, season phases. Principles aren't copyrightable; we already
  cite them from primary sources and public golf-fitness clinics.
- **Weekly speed tracking** as the progress signal (everyone does it).
- **Their evaluation rubric itself** — useful on our own landing page to reframe the
  comparison onto nutrition + AI, where we win.
- **Swing-measurement apps as future partners, not just rivals** — they generate swing
  data they can't coach on; we're the coaching layer. A later integration play.

**Avoid — proprietary data, branded protocols, and written content:**
- **Trademarked names**: "Golf Fitness Handicap™", "The DRVN Method™", branded
  protocol names ("SuperSpeed" stick weights/rep schemes), etc. Never use or imitate.
- **Proprietary biomechanics models / datasets**: Sportsbox's 3D skeleton, HackMotion's
  wrist-angle data — don't replicate branded numbers or scrape their data.
- **Competitor copy, exercise libraries, or program text** verbatim. Re-derive from
  primary sources (we already do).

**The clean line:** borrow the *science and the proven UX patterns*; never copy a
competitor's *data, branded protocol, or written content*.

## Handling the periodisation criticism

Competitor rubrics weight periodisation heavily; our evidence-based stance is that
**concurrent training beats block-switching when volume is equated.** We neutralise the
criticism without abandoning the science:

- **Keep the concurrent engine** (one consistent week training all qualities — the
  research-backed core).
- **Surface the season phases we already prescribe** — off-season build → in-season
  maintain → peak/taper — so a skeptic can't say we "just rotate workouts." We already
  have the in-season + peaking guidance; this is mostly a presentation change.
- **Defend the choice in plain language**: "We don't make you cycle through blocks
  because the research says you don't need to" — contrarian, honest, and true.

## Closing the one gap: the "Yardsmith Score"

A single composite athleticism/readiness number — our answer to DRVN's headline
assessment, built entirely from **our own data**, so there's no IP overlap.

- **Inputs (all already captured or easily added):**
  - 7-iron clubhead-speed trend (the headline golf signal)
  - Strength progression (key-lift estimated 1RMs from the log)
  - Power-to-weight (strength/speed relative to bodyweight — our golf framing)
  - Mobility/readiness self-check (a short rotational-mobility screen)
  - Consistency (training + macro adherence)
- **Output:** one number (e.g. 0–100) the golfer improves toward, with a breakdown of
  the contributing pillars and what to work on next — which the AI coach then explains.
- **Why it fits us:** it ties our nutrition, training, and speed data into one progress
  story, gives the app a north-star metric, and matches the one measurability edge the
  competition has — without copying their scoring or their trademark.
- **Phase:** target Phase 2/3 (needs the normalized data tables and the coach). See
  `ROADMAP.md`.
